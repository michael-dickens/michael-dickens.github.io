Who am I, and What do I do?

I'm Michael Dickens, and I am a student studying Human Geography, Cartography, and Geographic Information Systems at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

My primary academic interests involve the changes that take place across space, and how specific spaces are involved. GIS and Cartography help me realize those interests by providing a method of creating a visual product to help tell the story. My Human Geography background helps me find many new angles to look at a specific problems.

My personal interests involve sports and food. Whenever I find the opportunity, I love attending and watching sporting events especially football and hockey. Also when my life's journey takes me to a new locale, I like to try out as much of the unique foods around before I leave.

To tie my academic and personal interests together, I like to look at how space, place, and time affect the environment around sports through processes like expansion and relocation. I also like to look into things like history to see how spatial context affected the development of the foods we see today.

With the skills that I've learned through my coursework, I hope to be doing spatial analysis using these GIS skills and creating visuals to tell the story of my analysis through maps.
